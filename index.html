Occupational Therapy Explained

OTs help their clients find function, balance and satisfaction in life. An OT will focus on your physical, social and emotional well-being. Every person is completely unique. Your OT can help you engage in what ever matters most to you and meets your personal needs. For example: How to do your hair if you have a broken arm, overcoming anxiety with riding the city bus or finding the joy in baking again.

OTs most often work in clinical and school settings along side other professionals such as Physical and Speech Therapists. Through my years as a practitioner, I have realized that OT is not just for the sick, injured and disabled. There are so many layers of a human being to uncover and I believe that achieving true health and happiness is an ongoing process. Even people who consider themselves healthy can benefit from OT. There are always ways make life easier, more satisfying and fulfilling and I've decided to put a different spin on traditional OT and use it to its full potential.

wellness in the work place

We humans are extremely complex and adaptable. We’re able to adapt to challenging environments and that’s a valuable survival skill. However, adapting to prolonged periods of stress is not healthy. It can lead to disease and dysfunction. The demands of the modern world can be overwhelming and lead otherwise healthy adults to adapt by developing unhealthy life habits.

Technology can take a lot of the blame. Don't get me wrong, I'm grateful for technology. You wouldn't be expanding your mind on my website right now if it weren't for modern technology. Ha. Ha. Although technology is a crucial component of daily life, I am big believer in creating balance between the physcial and virtual world. These days it’s possible to make hundreds or thousands of virtual connections with real people every day. But, ironically, those connections, along with the pace and demands of everyday life can severely distract us from our true physical, emotional, and spiritual selves, our work responsibilities, our environment and each other. For example, do you remember what your breakfast tasted like? Do you remember your drive to work this morning? How meaningful were your face to face interactions today? Did you acknowledge any strangers? Do you often feel scattered and distracted? By reserving a few moments every day to consider our own existence and surroundings, we’re able to become more present and improve our interactions, the quaility of our work and our state of mind. Mindfulness and stress resiliance have the power to improve your relationship with yourself, your loved-ones, and your coworkers. It also has the potential to prolong the duration and quailty of your life!

I can help you and your colleagues learn strategies to slow down and become more intentional with your time and energy.

By having a dynamic and comprehensive wellness program in place you, your colleagues, employees and your company will be set up for success! Wellness programs are proven to increase overall work satisfaction, performance, productivity and employee retention. There is also potential for improved overall health which could mean less sick days and lower health insurance premiums. Show your staff that you value their work and their health by investing where it matters...in them!
